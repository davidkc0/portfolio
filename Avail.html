Avail

May 2019- Present | Skills used: Product design, UI/UX, Branding, Product management, User Research, Marketing, Team managment | URL: availcarsharing.com

Avail (formally Drift), is a car-sharing skunkworks started by Allstate insurance. The Avail team has been given a lot of freedom to innovate on the simple idea that "If you own a car, you should be able to put it to work while you're not using it." while at the same time utilizing the Allstate brand to position ourselves as the most trusted and safest way to share vehicles and make cars more accessible to everyone. I was brought on as the first internal Product Designer after the initial MVP had been created by an outside consulting company.

Avail about us page


My first major project at Avail was to design the About Us page. As a subsidiary of Allstate, one of the challenges with the About Us page and the brand, in general, was how to humanize it. We wanted Avail to feel like a welcoming and scrappy startup. One of the ways we achieved this was by including pictures of our lot staff and photos taking by our users. The center design was eventually chosen after we A/B tested the three. We found that that design felt both the most welcome and informative to the user.

Flare landing page


Flare is a secondary program within Avail that was created as a way for Allstate insurance users to be quickly given replacement cars. At the same time, their vehicle is being repaired after an accident. To be able to quickly test the hypothesis that this would be a better system than the current one used by Allstate, I was tasked with making a simple, yet beautiful landing page that succinctly explained the concept and allowed users to book their replacement car all in one page.
