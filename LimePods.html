LimePods

Sep 2018- May 2019 | Skills used: Product design, UI/UX, Branding, Product management, User Reasearch | URL: li.me

In the summer of 2018, Lime decided to diversify their fleet of dockless vehicles further to include cars. I was tapped to lead the design efforts for this major project.

Seattle: This was the first major product that we were able to incorporate user research into the process from the very beginning. The product and design team went to Seattle, WA, to conduct user interviews with competing services to try to figure out the space a bit better. We also took the time to try out these services for ourselves. We came away with vital information that set the right ground for the success of this project.

Based on user feedback, we decided that the main differentiating feature of LimePods would be the ability to reserve them. The feature was not yet included in ReachNow and had only just been added to Car2Go. It was important that reserving a LimePod would be easy and intuitive, with no hidden fees like Car2Go's version.

The reservation feature was so popular with LimePod riders that it was rolled out to scooters as well. This has been one of the most successful and praised features that have added, with one rider going so far as to say that it changed her life because it made her commute so much easier.
